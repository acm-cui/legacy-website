CUI@CHI: Ethics of Conversational User Interfaces

A workshop at the ACM CHI 2022 conference

Accepted position papers

The following papers were accepted for inclusion in the workshop:

  • Let’s talk about it: Spoken conversational search with a robot for children
    Thomas Beelen, Khiet P. Truong, Ella Velner, Roeland Ordelman, Vanessa Evers, and Theo Huibers
  • Using Conversational Artificial Intelligence to Support Children’s Search in the Classroom
    Garrett Allen, Jie Yang, Maria Soledad Pera, and Ujwal Gadiraju
  • Who Needs a Therapist? Teens’ Reactions to the Conversational Wellbeing Agent
    Irene Lopatovska
  • Designing a Disembodied Conversational Agent as a Digital Companion for Older Adults
    Ahmad Nurhadi, Simon Perrault, and Kenny Tsu Wei Choo
  • Building an interactive storytelling conversational agent through parent-AI collaboration
    Zheng Zhang, Ying Xu, Yanhao Wang, Bingsheng Yao, Daniel Ritchie, Tongshuang Wu, Mo Yu, Dakuo Wang, and Toby Jia-Jun Lib
  • Media Characters in Inclusive CUIs for Preschoolers
    Jennifer Rodriguez and Silvia Lovato
  • Incorporating Sociolects in CUIs for Children and Families
    Shun Yi Yeo and Simon T. Perrault

A CHI 2022 workshop bringing together researchers from the CUI and CHI communities to explore the ethics of conversational user interfaces.
